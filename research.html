Research Hubs

Research is the organizing logic of the College.

The Crestone College supports small, high-density research teams working across health, technology, environment, and social systems.

Hub I

Health Sciences & Human Performance

This hub focuses on human health, wellbeing, public health, preventive health, human performance, recovery, mental health, digital health, longevity research, and personalized interventions.

Representative Platform

Center for Health, Wellbeing & Human Performance supports research on preventive health, digital health tools, human performance assessment, and health behavior.

Hub II

AI, Data & Society

This hub studies artificial intelligence, data science, responsible AI, human-AI interaction, computational social science, AI for health, AI for education, and data responsibility in public systems.

Representative Platform

Applied AI & Data Lab supports applied AI research across health, education, public policy, organizational systems, and community resilience.

Hub III

Environment, Resilience & Human Futures

This hub studies climate resilience, environmental systems, sustainable cities, water and land systems, urban transformation, scenario planning, human adaptation, and long-term wellbeing.

Representative Platform

Resilience & Futures Lab supports research on climate resilience, community innovation, urban transformation, and future scenarios.

Cross-Hub Research Themes

Where the hubs meet.

Digital Health and Human Performance

AI health coaching, behavior change, health data, digital wellbeing, and ethics of performance tracking.

Climate, Health, and Community Resilience

Climate stress, public health, heat exposure, air quality, disaster readiness, and vulnerable populations.

AI for Resilient Communities

Responsible AI for climate adaptation, community planning, public decision systems, and resilience dashboards.

Human Futures Research Studio

Human wellbeing in technological society, future work, emerging systems ethics, and long-term human development.
