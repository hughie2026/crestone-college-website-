Academics

Research pathways built around practice, judgment, and synthesis.

The diploma portfolio is intentionally focused: one undergraduate-level cross-hub pathway and three graduate-level pathways aligned with the Academic Hubs.

01

Four diploma pathways

Each pathway combines seminars, methods work, research studios, portfolio development, and a substantial applied project.

01
Undergraduate level

Human Futures & Interdisciplinary Research

A college-wide introduction to health, AI and data, environmental resilience, ethics, systems thinking, and future-oriented inquiry.

Suggested duration: 4 academic years
Completion: Interdisciplinary Research Portfolio
02
Graduate level

Health Sciences & Human Performance

Public health, preventive health, mental wellbeing, human performance, digital health, health data, and longevity research.

Suggested duration: 1.5 academic years
Completion: Applied Health Research Project
03
Graduate level

AI, Data & Society

Artificial intelligence and data systems studied as technical, ethical, organizational, and social forces.

Suggested duration: 1.5 academic years
Completion: Applied AI & Society Project
04
Graduate level

Environment, Resilience & Human Futures

Climate resilience, environmental systems, sustainable cities, community adaptation, and long-term futures.

Suggested duration: 1.5 academic years
Completion: Resilience & Futures Research Project

Education model

Students participate in the research community.

Learning is organized through research studios, close reading, methods training, ethical analysis, collaborative inquiry, and public-facing translation.

StudioIterative project development with faculty guidance.
MethodsQualitative, quantitative, computational, and systems approaches.
EthicsJudgment about data, participation, risk, and public impact.
CapstoneA substantial applied research project or portfolio.
