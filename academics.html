Academic Programs

Diploma pathways grounded in research practice.

The Crestone College offers a focused diploma structure aligned with its three Academic Hubs and cross-hub research themes.

Diploma System

Four focused academic pathways.

Undergraduate Level

Undergraduate Diploma in Human Futures & Interdisciplinary Research

A college-wide pathway introducing health sciences, AI and data, environmental resilience, ethics, systems thinking, and future-oriented research.

  • Suggested duration: 4 academic years
  • Format: Hybrid / low-residency / research workshops
  • Completion: Interdisciplinary Research Portfolio
Graduate Level

Graduate Diploma in Health Sciences & Human Performance

Designed for learners interested in public health, mental health, preventive health, human performance, digital health, and health data.

  • Suggested duration: 1.5 academic years
  • Hub: Health Sciences & Human Performance
  • Completion: Applied Health Research Project
Graduate Level

Graduate Diploma in AI, Data & Society

Studies AI and data systems as technical, ethical, and social forces affecting health, education, governance, work, and public life.

  • Suggested duration: 1.5 academic years
  • Hub: AI, Data & Society
  • Completion: Applied AI & Society Project
Graduate Level

Graduate Diploma in Environment, Resilience & Human Futures

Focuses on environmental change, climate resilience, sustainable communities, urban transformation, and long-term human futures.

  • Suggested duration: 1.5 academic years
  • Hub: Environment, Resilience & Human Futures
  • Completion: Resilience and Futures Research Project

Education Model

Students as participants in a research community.

Education at The Crestone College is research-centered. Students engage in research studios, literature analysis, methods training, capstone work, and applied projects connected to public needs.

Each diploma pathway emphasizes research literacy, ethical judgment, interdisciplinary problem solving, and practical application.

Learning Components

  • Research studios and workshops
  • Interdisciplinary seminars
  • Applied methods training
  • Portfolio and capstone projects
  • Public-purpose research translation
