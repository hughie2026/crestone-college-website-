About the College

A small research college built for interdisciplinary work.

The Crestone College is located in Denver, Colorado, and focuses on health sciences, medical AI, data society, environmental resilience, and human futures.

Overview

Small in scale, high in research intensity.

The College is designed around a minimal-scale research university model. It does not depend on large schools, departments, or administrative layers. Instead, it organizes research, teaching, public projects, and partnerships through three interdisciplinary Academic Hubs.

This structure allows the institution to remain focused, flexible, and deeply research-oriented while supporting applied projects in health, technology, society, and environment.

Denver provides an urban research setting connected to health, technology, energy, environment, policy, and community transformation.

History

From Crestone Lab to The Crestone College.

1989

Crestone Lab

The institution’s intellectual origin traces back to Crestone Lab, a small independent research laboratory focused on human development, scientific exploration, environmental change, and knowledge innovation.

Early Development

Open Research Platform

Crestone Lab brought together researchers, educators, engineers, thinkers, and social practitioners to explore how knowledge could directly serve health, resilience, and future society.

Today

The Crestone College

The College continues the experimental spirit of Crestone Lab through Academic Hubs, research-based education, lean administration, and public-purpose research.

Mission, Vision, Values

Responsible inquiry for human futures.

Mission

To advance human health, responsible technology, environmental resilience, and social wellbeing through interdisciplinary research, research-based education, and public practice.

Vision

To become an internationally minded small research college focused on the most critical questions shaping healthy, resilient, and responsible human futures.

Core Values

Small by Design, Research with Public Purpose, Interdisciplinary by Default, Human-Centered Innovation, and Ethical Inquiry.
